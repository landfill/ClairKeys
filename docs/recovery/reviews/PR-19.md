## Scope

- Fixes issue [#18](https://github.com/landfill/ClairKeys/issues/18): long scores fell silent after ~10s while notes kept falling.
- Root cause: `src/hooks/useFallingNotesAudio.ts` scheduled audio once at play time and hard-capped it to notes starting within 10s (`relativeStart > 10`), never re-filling while playing.
- Replaces the one-shot scheduler with a rolling look-ahead scheduler:
  - New pure module `src/utils/audioScheduler.ts` (`selectNotesInWindow`, `nextScheduleWindow`, `SCHEDULE_AHEAD_SEC`, `TICK_MS`, `VOICE_LIMIT`) isolates the window-selection decision from AudioContext side effects.
  - `useFallingNotesAudio` now advances a song-time cursor on a 100ms timer, keeps `SCHEDULE_AHEAD_SEC` of audio queued, maps song time to AudioContext time through a single shared anchor, and carries voice/polyphony bookkeeping across ticks.
  - seek/tempo/mute changes keep funnelling through `startAudio`, which tears down the timer and re-anchors so future nodes cannot double-fire. Public hook API unchanged; only caller `useFallingNotesPlayer` needs no edits.
- Corresponds to P0-C Work stages 1–3. Work stages 4 (visual/key-activation share the same clock) and 5 (long-run drift measurement) are out of scope and remain `NOT_STARTED`.
- Dependency-order deviation (P0-C started before P0-A/P0-B) recorded as D-007 in `docs/recovery/DECISIONS.md`, committed on this PR's branch with the fix per `AGENTS.md`.
